• Points of Controversy

9.7 Of Future Ideas

Controverted Point: That a consciousness, having an idea that is future, is without object.

PTS cs 1.1.1–2These §§ are verbatim as in PTS CS 9.6, “future” substituted for “past”.

Theravādin: PTS cs 1.1.3continues: You admit of course concerning what is present, that there can be adverting of mind, ideation, and so on (PTS CS 9.6.2), so that consciousness of a present idea has its mental object. And you admit that there can be adverting of mind and the rest about the past and also about the future. Yet in both these cases mind, you say, is without mental object. PTS cs 1.1.4 Why not also say then that, while there can be adverting of mind, etc., about the present, mind occupied about a present object is mind without object?

Andhaka: PTS cs 1.1.5But you admit that a “past object” does not exist at the present moment? Surely then a mind occupied with past object is occupied with no (that is, with a non-existent) object … .
